Variable aperture is not something that has been on a smartphone camera for a very long time. In the past, "variable" apertures have been achieved by post-processing of an image and always added a lot of image noise. Today, some phones are putting a real variable aperture in their phones in order to create sharper low-light images.

An example of a modern phone with REAL variable aperture is the Samsung Galaxy S9, S9+ and the Note 9.

The aperture is the name given to the amount of light that is allowed to get onto the sensor of the camera.

Very few smartphones actually properly support this, and a lot of the time the aperture is software controlled. If your smartphone supports it, REAL variable aperture will be available in our own camera app. When you toggle 'SHOW APERTURE', it will show at the top of the screen, and you will be able to adjust it (within the parameters of your smartphone).
